You should clean the contacts with a soft cloth and let it dry for at least 48 hours. That should be enough.

Inside components cannot be damaged by being submerged in water if they aren't connected to a power supply (in such case they could short-circuit).

The only negative thing that could happen is that water could contain residue and, after the water evaporates, that residue could be conductive and lead to short-circuits, when you reconnect the device to a power supply.

But that is possible only if you drop it in very dirty water. In such cases, if the info on the USB drive is very important to you, take the devices to someone who knows how to take it apart and clean it in the inside. It won't look pretty anymore but you will be able to copy all the info somewhere else.
